HAWTHORNE AND HIS MOSSES. by a virginian spending july in vermont. A papered chamber in a fine old farm-house, a mile from any other dwelling, and dipped to the eaves in foliage--surrounded by moun-tains, old woods, and Indian ponds,--this, surely, is the place to write of Hawthorne. Some charm is in this northern air, for love and duty seem both impelling to the task. A man of a deep and noble nature has seized me in this seclusion. His wild, witch-voice rings through me; or, in softer cadences, I seem to hear it in the songs of the hill-side birds that sing in the larch trees at my window.
