239 250 The First Lieutenant taking judicial primacy and turning toward the prisoner, said, "Captain Vere has spoken. Is it or is it not as Captain Vere says?" In responce came syllables not so much impeded in the utterance as might have been anticipated. They 55 were these: "Captain Vere tells the truth. 14 It is just as Captain Vere says, but it is not as the Master-at-Arms said. I have eaten the King's bread.^ and I am true to the King." "I beleive you, my man" said the sole witness in his voice indicating a suppressed emotion not otherwise betrayed.
