288 299 ––//–– In a seventy-four of the old order the deck known as the upper gun-deck was the one covered over by the spar-deck which last tho'though not without 62 its armament was for the most part exposed to the weather. In general it was at all hours free from hammocks; those of the crew swinging on the lower gun-deck, and berth-deck, underneath that and next above the orlop-deck the latter being not only a dormitory but also the place for the stowing of the sailors' bags, and as well and on both sides lined with the ^large chests or movable pantries of the many messes of the men.
